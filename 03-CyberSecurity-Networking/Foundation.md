# 🌐 Network Types and Security

## 📌 Introduction

A **network** is a group of connected devices that communicate and share data and resources.

In cybersecurity, networks are important because attackers often target network communication to gain unauthorized access, steal data, or disrupt services.

---

# 🔌 Wired Network

A wired network uses physical cables to connect devices.

## Types of Wired Media:

### 1. Ethernet Cable

- Most common wired networking technology
- Used in homes, offices, and data centers
- Provides reliable and fast communication

---

### 2. Fiber Optic Cable

- Uses light signals to transmit data
- Extremely fast communication
- Used for long-distance connections and internet backbone

---

### 3. Coaxial Cable

- Uses copper conductor for data transmission
- Used in cable internet and television networks

---

## Advantages of Wired Networks:

✅ More secure  
✅ High speed  
✅ Stable connection  
✅ Less interference  
✅ Low latency  

---

## Disadvantages:

❌ Limited mobility  
❌ Requires physical installation  
❌ Difficult to move devices  

---

# 📡 Wireless Network

A wireless network uses radio waves to connect devices without cables.

## Examples:

- Wi-Fi
- Bluetooth
- Mobile Data (4G/5G)

---

## Advantages:

✅ Freedom to move  
✅ Easy installation  
✅ Supports mobile devices  

---

## Disadvantages:

❌ More vulnerable to attacks  
❌ Depends on signal strength  
❌ Connection can be affected by walls and interference  
❌ Lower stability compared to wired networks  

---

# 🛡️ Network Security in Cybersecurity

Network security protects data, devices, and communication from unauthorized access and attacks.

## Main Goals:

### 🔍 Detecting Attacks

Security teams monitor networks for:

- Suspicious traffic
- Unauthorized access
- Malware activity

---

### 🛡️ Protecting Data

Methods:

- Encryption
- Secure protocols
- Access control

---

### 🔒 Securing Networks

Using:

- Firewalls
- IDS/IPS
- VPN
- Network segmentation

---

### 👥 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Users receive permissions based on their job roles.

Example:

- Employee → Basic access
- Administrator → System management access

---

# 🌍 Types of Networks

---

# 🏠 LAN (Local Area Network)

LAN connects devices within a small geographical area.

Examples:

- Home network
- Office network
- School building

Range:

- Usually within a building or small area

Speed:

- High speed (100 Mbps to 10+ Gbps)

Characteristics:

✅ Fast  
✅ Easy to manage  
✅ More secure compared to larger networks  

---

# 🏙️ MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)

MAN covers a larger area than LAN but smaller than WAN.

Examples:

- City network
- University campus network
- Organization branches within a city

Managed by:

- Organizations
- Internet Service Providers (ISPs)

Example:

- Jio
- Airtel

Characteristics:

- Medium to high speed
- More complex than LAN
- Requires more security management

---

# 🌎 WAN (Wide Area Network)

WAN connects networks over very large geographical areas.

Examples:

- Internet
- Global company networks

Managed by:

- Internet Service Providers (ISPs)

Characteristics:

❌ Harder to control  
❌ More security challenges  
✅ Connects worldwide locations  

---

# 📊 Network Comparison

| Network | Coverage | Example | Security |
|---|---|---|---|
| LAN | Small area | Home, Office | Easier to control |
| MAN | City/Campus | ISP or University network | Moderate control |
| WAN | Worldwide | Internet | Most difficult to manage |

---

# ⭐ Key Concept
LAN → Small Area → High Speed → Easy Management

MAN → City Area → Medium Complexity

WAN → Worldwide → Complex Security

---

# 🔐 Network Security Challenges

Common threats:

- Unauthorized access
- Malware attacks
- Data interception
- DDoS attacks
- Man-in-the-Middle attacks
- Wireless attacks

Security professionals protect networks through:

- Monitoring
- Detection
- Prevention
- Incident response
